So what is it?

It's a quick way to pull together metadata essential to calculating a work's compliance with an Open Access policy.

And what's the benefit of that?

1. Effort reduction
Lantern can process a few thousand identifiers in a few hours. How much time to do manually?

2. Consistency and accuracy.
Results are easy to check, review and report on.

And why is this even necessary?

Increasingly there are OA mandates and policies appearing

  • Governmental - e.g. the UK's REF OA requirements
  • Funder - 79 policies registered in ROARMAP, e.g. Wellcome Trust
  • Institutional - 557 policies registered in ROARMAP

And alongside those policies, there is the need for librarians/research offices/funders to report on and enforce them.

An OA policy that can't be enforced isn't of any value

How does it work?

Upload your identifiers

Identifiers can consist of:

  • DOIs
  • PMIDs - PubMed
  • PMCIDs - Europe PMC

We'll take those as a spreadsheet, or if you're keen you can integrate with the API!

Go put your feet up

Even though it's quick, Lantern still takes a bit of time.

It's going to go out to a range of places on the web to gather your compliance information. Right now thats:

  • CrossRef - for essential biblio data
  • Europe PMC - for biblio data and fulltext/licence information
  • CORE - for repository deposits
  • Sherpa - for embargo and policies
  • DOAJ - for journal type
  • Grist - for grants and PIs
  • Publisher websites - to page-scrape licence information

When it's done, it'll email you

Download your results

You can download your results as a spreadsheet, and it'll contain the following things:

  • Some additional basic bibliographic information, such as Journal, Publisher, Title, and publication dates
  • Whether the publication is in a repository (and which repositories)
  • Whether the publication appears in EuropePMC, and some information about its status and version
  • What re-use licence the publisher has applied
  • Any embargo information about this publisher
  • Any self-archiving policies for the pre-print, post-print or publisher's version
  • Grant and PI information
Enforce your policy!

Well, maybe "enforce" is too strong a word for higher education.

But you'll be able to review the results with the help of detailed provenance information Lantern provides, and determine how well your mandate compliance is going, and whether you need to have a quiet word with a couple of departments.

Ok, let's talk about business models

Question

What's the correct community-focussed business model for services to Open Access?

There are a lot of possible answers to this question. We considered many, including: Feature-based Freemium, Usage-based Freemium, Public Good/Free, One cost for all, Tiered costs, Sponsorship, Membership and Grant Funded.

Our Answer

Usage-based Freemium

Secures access to the service for everyone in the community, and additional scale at cost for anyone that needs it, with a clean and clear business model.

So, how do we engage the community?

Help us manage licence statements dataset

You may remember the Open Article Gauge (it no longer exists). It was developed by Cottage Labs with funding from PLOS to page-scrape licence information from publisher websites

It used a long list of known licence statements. Those statements could be contributed by the community, but it was difficult to do, and the pool of contributors was small.

Instead, right now in Lantern we're managing this dataset internally.

Can we find a better way for the community to give us feedback, or new data? What is that mechanism? (seriously, we don't know yet).

Drive metadata standards

There are some key areas where compliance data is hard to gather.

  • Licence expressions - we've got a pretty good handle on this for the common (CC) licence types, but still publishers could make our lives easier
  • Funder Acknowledgemets - there's no standard expression for these, but they are important measures
  • Research Data Citations - there's no standard expression for these

Can we help bring together a community effort to improve this metadata?

Give us feature requests

We've got a few big things on the list, but we're always looking for more:

  • More information about items in repositories
  • More datasources (e.g. BASE, Gateway to Research)
  • Results visualisation
  • Automatic compliance calculations

If you have any, email us, or look at our public issue tracker
